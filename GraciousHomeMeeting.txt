### Summary of Meeting between Gracious Home Health Care Services and Vitable Health

**Participants:**
- Erika Reynolds (Vitable Health)
- Rachel Azodo (Gracious Home Health Care Services)
- Omo (Gracious Home Health Care Services)

**Main Topics Discussed:**
1. **Current Benefits Situation:**
   - Gracious Home Health Care currently does not have a healthcare benefits plan in place for their employees.
   - Omo learned about Vitable Health through a colleague, and expressed interest in exploring benefits for their staff, who consist of about 21 full-time employees.
   - The company does not currently handle TB testing internally; instead, employees are instructed to use urgent care or visit their primary care doctors to complete the requirement.

2. **Motivation for Considering New Benefits:**
   - The organization is looking to implement healthcare benefits as a means of improving employee satisfaction and retention.
   - They expressed interest in understanding more about Vitable Health’s offerings, specifically how they could provide essential healthcare services at a reasonable cost.

3. **Interest in Vitable Health’s Offerings:**
   - Erika presented Vitable Health’s primary care and Minimum Essential Coverage (MEC) plans, explaining the range of services offered, such as primary care visits, urgent care, and mental health support.
   - A discussion was held on the costs of primary care for the employer ($30 per employee per month) and the specifics of coverage included in MEC and MEC Plus plans.
   - The group also showed interest in Vitable’s TB testing program, which offers a convenient process for employees through partnerships with Labcorp and Quest Diagnostics.

4. **Next Steps:**
   - Erika proposed a follow-up meeting to dive deeper into the available plans and costs, including a product demonstration to help the team at Gracious Home Health Care better understand the offerings.
   - Erika will bring her boss Brian to the next meeting to provide additional expertise and answer detailed questions.

### Company Information:

1. **Company Name:** Gracious Home Health Care Services
2. **Company Industry:** Home Health Care
3. **Company Size:** Approximately 21 full-time employees
4. **Previous Knowledge of the Affordable Care Act:** Limited knowledge, with a need for guidance on ACA compliance as the company grows.
5. **Core Challenges:**
   - Lack of a current healthcare benefits plan for employees.
   - Need for a cost-effective solution that offers essential healthcare coverage.
6. **What They're Looking For:**
   - A basic healthcare benefits plan that includes primary care, prescription coverage, and TB testing.
   - A flexible, affordable plan to help attract and retain employees.

### Next Steps Suggested:
- Vitable Health to schedule a follow-up meeting with a more detailed overview of plan options and a product demo.
- Erika to provide further details on TB testing costs and the specific services covered under each plan.
